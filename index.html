CASE STUDY

PLAN & SIMPLE MOBILE APP

Planning group trips can be challenging due to disagreements among travelers, causing stress and discomfort. The solution is an app called Plan and Simple, which was developed through user research and competitor analysis. The app aims to simplify the group travel planning process by offering intuitive features based on multiple rounds of wireframes and user testing, although it is not yet fully developed.

Steps

The Process

Overview

The process of this project included: creating a proto persona, planning, conducting, and recording interviews, piecing together findings from interviews through an affinity diagram, empathy map, and user persona, generating a user insight statement, creating a value proposition, putting together a storyboard/user insight statement, conducting 3 competitor analysis, creating a user flow chart, sketching wireframes, making paper prototypes, creating low fidelity wireframes, testing prototypes with users, and iterating low fidelity into high fidelity based on user testing.

User Research & Findings

Step 1

In this research project, I planned and conducted 5 initial interviews to research what kind of travel app users are looking for, and 4 user tests on prototypes that consisted of 3 tasks to go through.

Ideation Design

Step 2

The ideation design process began with sketching out wireframes that were then brought onto the computer and turned into a clickable paper prototype. Findings from user tests conducted with paper prototypes were then measured and used to move into the low fidelity design phase.

Usability Testing & Findings

Step 3

Usability testing was conducted to find out what worked and what the pain points were in each step of the process. For example, multiple users tested in the low fidelity phase thought one task was to change their password rather than reset it. Because of this confusion, I made the tasks clearer in how they were delivered and the updated wording on buttons, which led to more seamless results in the next round of testing.

Iteractive Prototype

Step 4

A total of 3 prototypes were created throughout the entire process: paper prototype, low fidelity prototype, and high fidelity prototype. It is important to focus first on the usability, and then add design elements later down the line.

Summary

Final Thoughts

While Plan & Simple is not entirely complete, it has a great start to an app that can solve the headache of planning group travel. My further development of the app would include creating the trip planning process, adding to ability to share trips with friends, building out the profile, and building out the home screen further. I would utilize user testing as I continue to build out Plan & Simple because intuitiveness and usability are key factors in creating a successful app that users will enjoy. The ultimate goal is to create an app that solves my user persona’s largest pain point: planning trips for group travel.
